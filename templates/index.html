Domain Knowledge

In patients with myocardial ischemia or infarction, findings on the ECG are influenced by multiple factors, including the following:

  1. Duration – Hyperacute/acute versus evolving/chronic
  2. Size – Amount of myocardium affected
  3. Anatomic location – Anterior, lateral, or inferior-posterior
  4. After a myocardial infarction, the first abnormality seen on the ECG is elevation of the ST segment.
  5. Subsequently, Q waves appear, and the T waves become inverted. The ST segment returns to the baseline, the whole process taking a variable time but usually within the range 24–48 h.
  6. T wave inversion is often permanent.
