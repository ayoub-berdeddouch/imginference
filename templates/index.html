Domain Knowledge

●ECG contains waves, intervals, segments and one complex

      ● Wave: A positive or negative deflection from baseline that indicates aspecific electrical event( P wave, Q wave, R wave, S wave, T wave andU wave)
      ●Interval: The time between two specific ECG events.(PR interval, QRSinterval, QT interval and RR interval)
      ● Segment: The length between two specific points on an ECG that aresupposed to be at the baseline amplitude ( PR segment, ST segmentand TP segment).
      ● Complex: The combination of multiple waves grouped together. Theonly main complex on an ECG is the QRS complex
●ECG is made up of 12 characteristic views of the heart, six obtained from the ‘limb’ leads (I, II, III, VR, VL, VF) and six from the ‘chest’ leads (V1–V6).
